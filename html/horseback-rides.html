TO THE LAND OF THE STARS

"To the land of the stars" horseback tour works as our first chain of the "Where the nomads are" tour and featuring five full horse riding days with local english speaking guide and starts from the shepherds farm at the foot of the Tien Shan mountains. Many livestock trails will take you along the rivers up the SonKul range where you finally get a spectacular view of the beautiful lake SonKul surrounded by thousands of Yurts.

Location

Central SonKul Range

Naryn province

North Kyrgyzstan

Duration

1 Week (5 full riding days. 2 days for road trip between Bishkek - SonKul lake - Bishkek)

Elevation

2Start point at 2400m in Kyzart village The highest point of the trip is will be the 3450m Jalgyz karagay pass The trekking ends by finishing the circle around the lake in the north shore of the SonKul 3016m The highest Yurt stay branched at 3016m

Season

Starts from mid June and stretches till the end of September.

Starting from

580 $

You don’t have to worry about complicated accesses. All the guests and their luggages will be transferred right to the front door of the lodges by vehicles. On the morning of the lodge to lodge touring day you just pack your stuff and it will wait for you at the next destination.

Day 1

Location: Meeting in Manas Airport/Bishkek – Transfer to the Kyzart village. Altitude: 900m to 2400m Tame taken: 5-6 hours, 270km Trek gradient: Non Water sources: Water bottles. Accomodation: House/Yurt You’ll be picked up at the airport and we’ll get you checked into the hotel to freshen up and rest after a long flight. You’ll have to time if you get in early to see a bit of a Bishkek before beginning our way to small Kyzart village. Make a turn to Burana tower where the starts historical excursion. According the timing do another break in Boom valley or in Kochkor village for a big lunch. Finally arriving to the village at the evening time when we get all together behind the family style dinner our guides will provide guests with a briefing about the upcoming trip, safety and everything what we should cover.

Day 2

Location: MeKilemche pasture. Altitude: 2400m to 3240m Tame taken: 3-4 hours, 15km Trek gradient: Easy Water sources: Carry sufficient water from Kyzart. Guest will find clean water from the springs or side valleys along the trail to refill water bottles. Accomodation: Yurt The second day starts right from the front door of the house and ends in the Kilemche jailoo (Carpet pasture) where you will have a rest in a host Yurt till next day. The day starts with equipping the horses/horse riding brief and follows with exploring morning life of the village while you will crossing it. The rest of the day will roll on the summer shepherds pasture with gentle flowing uphill in the valley.

Day 3

Location: North shore of the SonKul lake Altitude: 3240m to 3016m via 3450m Tame taken: 4-5 hours, 18km Trek gradient: Easy Water sources: Water bottles. Accomodation: Yurt Todays trekking provides with crossing one small pass of Jalgyz Karagay 3240m and features panoramic view of the lake in distant but as you ride down the slopes, it gets larger, the mountains on the opozit side gets higher until finally the lake fills most of your fieldview and the southern mountains tower above it. . Bigger part of the day will spended for uphill riding than slowly easy descent to the lake side for another starry night.

Day 4

Location: South shore of the lake. Altitude: 3016m Tame taken: 7-8 hours, 25km Trek gradient: Easy Water sources: Water bottles. Accomodation: Yurt This is a flat but long day just crossing lots of villages of the host and shepherds Yurts, riding along the lake with radial hikes for amazing views to the down bellow from the side passes.

Day 5

Location: Batai aral Yurt village Altitude: 3016m Tame taken: 8-9 hours, 35km Trek gradient: Easy Water sources: Water bottles. Accomodation: Yurt Still riding along the Lake for finish the circle. If you’ve been a first time on horseback at the beginning of the week today you believe you will galloping

Day 6

Location: Tuz Ashuu pass 3400m Altitude: 3016 m to 2500m via 3400m Tame taken: 6-7 hours Trek gradient: Easy Water sources: Water bottles. Accomodation: lodge Riding down the pasture via crossing the Tuz Ashu pass 3400m and drop to another side valley from the lake. Continuing trip with a 4x4 vehicle by crossing Kokomeren river to the other famous pasture Suusamyr valley.

Day 7

Location: Bishkek Altitude: 2500m to 900m via 3600m Tame taken: 4 hours, 260km Trek gradient: Non Water sources: Water bottles. Accomodation: Hotel Last day of the trip proposes early breakfast and roadtrip to the capital trough the famous Too Ashuu pass 3600m with in 3km tunnel. As soon as the group arrives to the capital our guides will take you down to the colorful Osh Bazar and the souvenir shops. The day will ends with a small city tour.

Included

  • Kyrgyz Tours Guides (lead guide + tail guide)

  • Airport transfers

  • Roundtrip transfer between Bishkek-Karakol

  • Hotel in Bishkek/lodging and meals in Karakol

  • lodges, Yurts, Tents with all meals and drinks at the riding time

  • Animal support/porters (if needed)

  • One satellite phone for the crew

Excluded

  • Visa support (Kyrgyzstan offers a visa free policy for most countries

  • International flights to and from Bishkek

  • Medical/evacuation insurance

Experience

For joining our trek you should have fit and adventurous nature. This trek doesn’t require advanced horse riding skills but you have to be confident spending up to 4 to 5 hours a day on the saddle, not get too mad on a rough weather condition. On the trail we have no 5 star hotels but yurts with wood/coal burning stoves.

Group size

6 person min – 12 person max. For all groups our services are works privately. We don’t like mess in our bases and we never mix 2 different groups in one. If the number of the guests are less than 6 either more than 12, shoot us an email for discussing for how we roll in that case

Insurance

Every one who joining us for skiing/trekking/horse riding in KG should have a personal MED-EVAC insurance which can be easily paired with Kyrgyz mountain rescue service.

Gear

Whole structure of the trekking and personal gear. Kyrgyz people never use helmet, riding breeches, gaiters or any other special equipments so bring everything what can make you more safe and happy on top of the horse.

Camping

Bring everything what you would bring if you are going for a multi days trekking, except tents and kitchen needs.

Horses & Riding

All the horses used for the tour are owned by the Kyrgyz tribes or shepherds and carefully selected by our horseman. They are usually local breeds such as Donskaya or Kyrgyz or crosses of the two, they vary in height from 14.1-15.3hh and are very surefooted, super fit and adapted to the altitude and mountainous terrain. The saddles are very simple, traditional Kyrgyz or Russian saddles. covered with a large and deep sheep skin cloth and pretty comfortable for all day rides. Saddle bags are available on request. The horses are usually all male, either stallions or geldings. The mares are used for breeding.

Group of 6-12

580 $/person/tour

Breakfasts

Are starts with a coffee which ends with omelettes, french toasts, pancakes, burritos, porages and lots of sweets

Lunches

Packed lunchboxes for the field

Apres ski

You are welcome to our dining yurt anytime especially after ski days to hang there for some tea with bred and cheese/meet slice, where we pull out the maps and discuss the routes for further days.

Drinks

Unlimited Tea, French press coffee, Home made fruit juice

Dinners

Lagman (Meat and vegetables with spicy sauce on top of a hand-stretched noodles). Plov (Rice with mutton and vegetables). Kuurdak (Fried beef/lam or chicken with potatoes). Shorpo (Soup with chunks of vegetables and lam). Oromo (vegetables and mints rolled in the pasta cooked on steam) Besh barmak (Lam, noodles, onions with a soup of a mutton). Dymdama (Vegetables with beef covered by cabbage and steamed in Kazan). Almost all the meals are have ability to be cooked as a vegetarian one.

Experience

For joining our trek you should have fit and adventurous nature. This trek doesn’t require advanced horse riding skills but you have to be confident spending up to 7 to 8 hours a day on the saddle.

Group size

6 person min – 12 person max. For all groups our services are works privately. We don’t like mess in our bases and we never mix 2 different groups in one. If the number of the guests are less than 6 either more than 12, shoot us an email for discussing for how we roll in that case

Insurance

Every one who joining us for skiing/trekking/horse riding in KG should have a personal MED-EVAC insurance which can be easily paired with Kyrgyz mountain rescue service.

Gear

Whole structure of the trekking and personal gear. Kyrgyz people never use helmet, riding breeches, gaiters or any other special equipments so bring everything what can make you more safe and happy on top of the horse.

Camping

Bring everything what you would bring if you are going for a multi days trekking, except tents and kitchen needs.

Horses & Riding

All the horses used for the tour are owned by the Kyrgyz tribes or shepherds and carefully selected by our horseman. They are usually local breeds such as Donskaya or Kyrgyz or crosses of the two, they vary in height from 14.1-15.3hh and are very surefooted, super fit and adapted to the altitude and mountainous terrain. The saddles are very simple, traditional Kyrgyz or Russian saddles. covered with a large and deep sheep skin cloth and pretty comfortable for all day rides. Saddle bags are available on request. The horses are usually all male, either stallions or geldings. The mares are used for breeding.
